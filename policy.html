Policy Inputs for entries enumerated under Schedule 7 of the Indian Constitution



  • Background: In 2021, India e-commerce retail segment has grown by 25% during the pandemic. India's e-commerce is concentrated, with few marketplaces holding a large portion of the consumer facing market share. Small sellers and businesses are becoming reliant on e-marketplace giants to reach e-commerce consumers. E-marketplace giants control entry into the e-commerce market and such act as 'gatekeepers'. E-marketplaces also play the dual role of being both an infrastructure provider and a competitor selling on the same platform. This has led to several competition issues in the P2P equation which include self-preferencing and deep discounting.
    Recommendation(s)
  • Emerging international regulatory consensus to selectively designate and regulate large incumbent 'gatekeeping' platforms.
  • The preferred modus operandi is through complementary ex-ante competition regulation that is aimed at preventing certain unfair conducts such as self-preferencing and anti-competitive leveraging, imposition of unfair contract terms such as most favoured nation clauses and exclusive selling, and to promote transparency in collection and usage of aggregated consumer data.
  • Tighter scrutiny of mergers and acquisitions involving gatekeepers by introducing new thresholds as well as new substantiative standards for such merger assessments.
  • The establishment of units either within existing regulators or as new authority to build expertise and aid enforcement in fast evolving digital markets.
    • Based on: Independent assessments by Vidhi Centre for Legal Policy
